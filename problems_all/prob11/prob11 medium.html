Your task is to count all the years within a given interval that share this peculiar property:
having just one zero in their binary representation.

Input:
** The input consists of a single line containing two integers 'x' and 'y' (1 ≤ x ≤ y ≤ $10^{14}$) — representing the first year and the last year of the interval.

Output:
** Print a single integer — the number of years within the chosen interval that have only one zero in their binary representation.

  • Example :
Input Output
100 105
 0
5 10
 2
  • Notes
      In the second sample, Limak's interval contains numbers \(5_{10} = 101_2\), \(6_{10} = 110_2\), \(7_{10} = 111_2\), \(8_{10} = 1000_2\), \(9_{10} = 1001_2\), and \(10_{10} = 1010_2\).
      Two of them (\(101_2\) and \(110_2\)) have the described property.
