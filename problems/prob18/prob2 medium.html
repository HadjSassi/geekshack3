• Bus travel - Maximum gain

A travel agency, long distance, wants to calculate the maximum possible gain of a trip (round trip) between 2 cities in relation to the fuel consumption of the bus, the number of passengers and the rates of the reserved tickets.

Knowing that:

- The bus tank can take up to F units fuel and for each unit of distance travelled, the bus consumes one unit of fuel.
- The distances between the N gas stations from the start of the route and the price per unit of fuel that each station offers, are known in advance.
- At the start, the bus tank is filled to T units.
- At each station visited, the tank will be filled to capacity.
- The number of A bus stops between the 2 cities and the fares from each stop to the final destination are, also, known & in advance. - From each stop B tickets are reserved.

Write a program that will accept the above information and calculate the maximum amount that can be gained from a trip (round trip). If the trip will not bring any gain, it will be cancelled.

Input:
The first line will contain 4 integers separated by spaces:
N (0 < N < 50001): The total number of service stations
F (0 <F <1000001): The units of fuel the bus can take
T (0 ≤ T ≤ F): The units of fuel initially contained in your tank
A (0 < N < 50001): The total number of bus stops (the arrival stop is not to be counted)

Each of the following N lines will contain 4 integers:

The first 2 correspond to the outward turn and the other 2 to the return turn.
The first of each couple, D_i(0 ≤ D_i ≤ L) corresponds to the distance of the station from the starting point, and the second, C_i (1 ≤ C_i) second, C_i (1 ≤ C_i ≤ 1,000,000) is the cost per unit of fuel for that station.


Each of the following A lines will contain 4 integers:

1) Nb_iA (1 ≤ Nb_iA ≤ 50) the number of tickets reserved from stop i (Go).
2) P_iA (1 ≤ P_iA ≤ 10000) the price of tickets from stop i (Go).
3) Nb_iR (1 ≤ Nb_iR ≤ 50) the number of tickets reserved from stop i (Return).
4) P_iR (1 ≤ P_iR ≤ 10000) the ticket price from stop i (Return).

Output:
You should have 3 lines; one for the amount to be paid for the purchase of fuel, one for the sum of the prices of the booked tickets and the other for the profit of the agency of the planned trip.

NOTES

You should assume the input exeption !

Don't forget that the order of the stops is the opposite of the one for the outward journey.
